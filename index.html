AUTH GUIDE

How to spot a fake Pokémon card

💡

Light Test

Hold the card up to a light source. Authentic cards show a distinct black inner layer. Fakes often let light through uniformly.

🎨

Print Quality

Genuine cards have sharp, crisp printing. Counterfeits often appear blurry, pixelated, or have washed-out colors on close inspection.

✏️

Font Accuracy

Check the fonts used for HP values, attack names, and card text. Fakes often use slightly wrong fonts, sizes, or spacing.

✨

Holographic Pattern

Real holo cards have complex, multi-layered patterns that shift with light. Fake foil is usually flat, uniform, and doesn't have the same depth.

📐

Card Weight & Feel

Genuine cards measure 63×88mm with a specific weight and texture. Fakes may feel too light, too thick, too glossy, or too rough.

🔢

Set Symbol & Rarity

Each card's set symbol should match the official set it belongs to. Cross-reference with the Pokémon card database to verify.

🔵

Card Back

The Pokémon card back has a very specific blue color and design. Fakes often have the wrong shade of blue or slightly off proportions.

💰

Price Red Flags

If a rare card is priced far below market value, be suspicious. Always buy from reputable sellers with authentication guarantees for high-value cards.
